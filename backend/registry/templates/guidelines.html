1. Government Orders (GOs)

All land transactions must comply with the latest government orders (GOs) released by the Revenue and Registration departments. Ensure the survey number and property boundaries are verified and not under legal dispute or public acquisition.

  • GO 121: E-Registration Mandate (2022)
  • GO 98: Encumbrance Certificate Mandatory
  • GO 33: Digital Survey Maps Legal Validity

2. Land Registration Rules

Land registration must follow the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 and the Indian Registration Act, 1908. The buyer and seller must be physically present or represented legally.

  • Seller must submit Aadhaar and property documents
  • Encumbrance certificate must be verified (past 13 years)
  • Stamp duty and registration charges apply as per slab
  • Fingerprint and photo verification mandatory

3. Required Documents

Make sure the following are ready before proceeding:

  1. Latest Property Tax Receipt
  2. Original Sale Deed or Gift Deed
  3. Encumbrance Certificate (Form 15/16)
  4. Survey and Location Map
  5. Buyer & Seller ID proofs (Aadhaar, PAN)

4. Digital Registration Process

Through the official portal, land registration is now digitized with officer login, document upload, biometric verification and real-time validation against fraudulent claims.

  • Officer logs in to verify documents
  • Data entered into secure land registry system
  • ML system flags possible duplicates
  • Receipt and certificate digitally signed
